# Authoring Pipeline Principles

KDNA Studio helps a person, Agent, tool, or mixed workflow make judgment
explicit, review its boundaries, and export a versioned `.kdna` file. It is an
authoring environment, not the authority that decides whether a judgment is
true or good.

## Author neutrality

A valid KDNA asset may be created by a human, AI system, organization, tool, or
mixed process. The authoring flow must declare its provenance accurately.

If an export claims to represent a named person or organization, that subject
must confirm the represented judgment. If it makes no such representation
claim, subject confirmation is not a Core validity requirement.

## Minimum workflow

1. identify the judgment and its intended scope;
2. express meaningful choices, boundaries, tradeoffs, and failure risks;
3. review and revise the content;
4. record accurate provenance and any claimed confirmation;
5. export one versioned `.kdna` file;
6. validate, plan, and load that exact file through Core.

AI assistance may interview, structure, challenge, or compile. It must not
invent human confirmation, hide uncertain provenance, or convert an external
assessment into a format-validity gate.

## Optional author review

An author may review a small set of previously unused situations to check that
the asset expresses the intended direction, respects its boundaries, and
defers correctly to current facts, user intent, law, safety, and Host
permissions. This is an author acceptance activity, not an intelligence or
superiority test.

## Studio boundary

Studio owns creation, editing, provenance capture, and export. It does not own:

- universal content approval or ranking;
- a mandatory registry or marketplace;
- Agent permissions or autonomous asset selection;
- organization-wide governance infrastructure;
- a guarantee that using an asset produces a preferred outcome.

Experimental authoring modules must remain outside the default public export,
CLI, and package surface until their own product need and contract are accepted.
